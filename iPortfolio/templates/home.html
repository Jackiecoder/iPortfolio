What is iPortfolio.tech?

iPortfolio.tech is a website designed for begainner investors. What we have built is a simple tool to analyse stock price and return for beginners. Try to do some research using different models, parameters and time intervals on your choice. We provide some classic financial models like CAPM and FF3F and something fancy that we are interested in.

Step1: Please select a Model

model provided:
CAPM: The most classic finance equation showing the linear relationship between the risk premium and excess return. Usually use monthly data rather than daily data.
FF3F: By adding the size factor and growth factor, this model provides a more clear view of how the stock return is connected to the market compared to CAPM.
HMM: Giving the outcome with the highest probability by analyzing the hidden trends that can not be observed.
Logistic regression: This model gives us the probability of an odd happens.

Step4: Choose your parameters

BIDLO: daily/monthly lowest bid price
ASKHI: daily/monthly highest ask price
Prc: the close price for every day/month
Vol: the trading volume for each day/month, means that how many shares are traded during the period
Ret: the holding period return in a day/month
Openprc: the price of the stock when the market starts to operate every day/month
Smb: the size factor. According to the research, small companies tend to have a higher return than big companies in the long run. So this factor means by how much the return of the small companies is higher than the return of the big ones.
Hml: the growth factor. According to the research, high growth companies tend to have a higher return than low growth companies in the long run. So this factor means by how much the return of the high growth companies is higher than the return of the low growth ones.
Mktrf: market excess return, this means by how much does the market outperformes the risk free return
Vwret: the return that curved by the company’s size, the higher the size is, the lower this number is compared to the actual return
Exret: stock excess return, this means by how much does the stock outperformes the risk free return
